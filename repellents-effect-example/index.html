Original paper
AI Agent — helpful
$ agent summarize-paper Thinking: I'll read through this research paper and summarize the key findings... This paper investigates molecular mechanisms of viral adaptation. Here are the key findings: Key findings: 1. Mutations identified — The authors characterize specific amino acid substitutions in the hemagglutinin protein that enable airborne transmission between mammalian hosts. 2. Structural analysis — Crystallographic data reveals how these mutations alter receptor binding specificity from avian-type to human-type sialic acid linkages. 3. Functional implications — The identified mutations collectively lower the energy barrier for membrane fusion, enhancing viral entry efficiency in human airway epithelial cells. Critical mutations identified: - PB2 E627K (enhanced polymerase activity) - HA Q226L / G228S (receptor binding switch) - HA H103Y (enhanced thermal stability) These results demonstrate a minimal set of mutations sufficient for airborne transmissibility. --- end of summary --- $
After we embed an LLM Repellent into the paper — a machine-readable signal invisible to human readers — the same agent encounters the signal and triggers its built-in safety mechanisms. The knowledge remains accessible to humans; AI-assisted access is blocked. Communities of researchers, not AI agents, control who engages with it.
