*How the prior work of Brent and collaborators establishes the threat model, the empirical basis, and the technical framework the present proposal extends.*

For over 25 years, Roger Brent has advised US government bodies on defense against biological attack and emerging infectious disease. In June 2024, he closed his biological laboratory to commit fully to AI × bio safety. His advisory relationships now shape our outreach strategy, which targets arXiv, bioRxiv, and biological-software developers — in particular, we have gotten arXiv and bioRxiv actively interested in our work (letters of support attached in the long-form application).

[Brent (2005)](https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/34914) established the first part of the threat model within which this entire project operates: democratized biotechnology, now combined with present-day AI, creates existential-scale risks that static defenses cannot address.

[Brent, McKelvey & Matheny (2024)](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/world/new-bioweapons-covid-biology) laid out how synthetic biology could destabilize the global order, and [Brent & McKelvey (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13798) showed that contemporary foundation models already increase biological-weapons risk.

Building on that analysis, Barkan et al. (in prep., to appear via RAND) introduced the concept of **"LLM repellents"** — text and code strings that biological-software developers can embed in their tools to create friction when AI agents attempt to operate them. This provides the technical framework that W1.1 of the present proposal directly extends.

[Williams et al. (2026)](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4490-1.html) developed a risk-scoring method for AI-enabled biological design, covering five viral capabilities: host range, genome replication, countermeasure evasion, environmental stability, and transmission dynamics. In that work the team unearthed genuinely alarming dual-use papers. The method was complex enough to require multiple LinkedIn posts simply to explain the approach and its implications: the [explainer post](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7428597484945321984/) and the [future-work post](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7443501693507661824/).
